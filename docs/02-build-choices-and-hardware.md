# Build Choices and Hardware

I kept telling myself this should be a quick hardware pick, but it really was not.

The bigger pattern was that I wanted gear that made sense now and still made sense later. I was not building this with a "one and done" mentality. I wanted parts I could reuse across future aircraft.

I also did not have a throw-money-at-it budget, so most decisions were value decisions. Not cheap for the sake of cheap, and not premium for the sake of premium. I wanted parts that were solid and worth the money.

What I actually ended up with was a SpeedyBee F405 Wing, ELRS + Boxer, dual 35A ESCs, twin F60-class motors, and a 4S baseline. On paper that sounds straightforward. In practice the challenge was not buying the parts. The challenge was getting everything to play nicely together.

The tiny hardware decisions were way more stressful than expected. Linkage fitment, horns, rods, clevises, connector choices, and wiring layout ended up taking more energy than the big-ticket items.

If I had to summarize my hardware logic in one line: buy once in a way that stays useful, but do not spend like reliability only comes from the highest price tag.
