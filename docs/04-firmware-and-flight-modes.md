# Firmware and Flight Modes

Firmware was one of those decisions that looked simple until I had to live with it.

I started with INAV because it was easier to get moving. As the build got more serious, I kept drifting toward ArduPilot because it matched the long-term direction better.

The part that matters most here is what I wanted from modes. I was not trying to hand the plane over to autonomy right away. I wanted support while I learned. FBWA made the most sense for that.

Most of my frustration came from the gap between what I thought a mode was doing and what the aircraft was actually doing. Switch positions, output mapping, arming behavior, and bench expectations all had to line up. If one part was off, confidence dropped immediately.

I came out of this with a much simpler rule: mode names are easy, mode validation is the real work.
